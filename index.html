About Me

I am a researcher and data scientist specializing in machine learning and AI, with a PhD in Sciences and Technologies of Information and Communication from Université Paris-Saclay, France. My work spans from applied AI research to hands-on data science and video streaming projects, making me uniquely equipped to drive innovation in both academic and industry settings. My background includes a wealth of experience in human-in-the-loop learning, active learning for autonomous systems, and safety-aware AI solutions. I have also collaborated internationally on AI-driven projects, such as video streaming optimization and analytics, contributing to impactful developments in adaptive video streaming and real-time data extraction pipelines. In my recent roles, including with CEA LIST and Upfluence SAS, I conducted research into video streaming trends and influencer marketing, studied consumer engagement in digital media, and developed machine learning techniques for autonomous and embedded systems. Additionally, I’ve presented my research at global conferences like SAFECOMP and IJCAI, publishing widely in areas such as active learning, data science and machine learning for video streaming. With technical expertise in Python, TensorFlow, and tools like Git and AWS, I am committed to pushing the boundaries of what’s possible with AI. I'm excited about continuing to bridge the gap between research and practical applications in AI, video streaming, and data science.
